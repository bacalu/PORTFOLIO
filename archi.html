OTHERS

The starting point of the project is the premise of the undeniable need for social housing in Romania, with the design primarily guided by the concept of the temporary user of this type of dwelling. The project is located in a developing area of the Fabric district in Timișoara, on a plot positioned at the threshold between collective housing developments and the beginning of single-family residential areas. The proposal consists of a ground floor plus three stories (GF+3), with an adjacent after-school function on the ground floor, addressing the most common profile of social housing users in Romania—namely, large families.

The upper floors are composed of two volumes containing the apartments, connected by a metal-structured walkway. This walkway is set back from the window areas, creating light courts and ensuring residents’ privacy. The edges of the walkway are defined by 4 mm stainless steel rods that support climbing vegetation; arranged in an irregular rhythm, the plants grow along the full height of the building, concealing the walkway from the view of passersby. At the corner of the plot, the rods extend over the entire height of the building and are evenly spaced at 10 cm, forming a screen for a designated area intended to encourage community interaction. The chosen colors for the walkway reflect the predominant palette of the Fabric district, while the exposed concrete-like walls and the cold metal of the rods reinterpret the language of modernist industrial design. The structure and layout of the apartments are designed to allow flexibility in the size of the living spaces according to the temporary users’ needs. This adaptability is emphasized in the interior design, which clearly delineates the zones that enable the apartments’ flexibility.
